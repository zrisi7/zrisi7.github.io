Pixverse

Pixverse brings an interactive web treasure hunt where the user is a curious and mischievous cat. They're able to collect stickers, wallpapers and relief their stress. Using HTML, CSS, javascript, Blender, A-frame & Illustrator

Based on the story of Pandora's Box.
Therefor, don't read further.

Overview

Pixverse brings an interactive web treasure hunt where the user is a witch's familiar (a curious and mischievous cat). They are able to collect digital downloads. In order to receive the next chapter, they have to sent a message to our Instagram account with the provided password. This helps us obtain metrics. Originally, there was a physical treasure hunt along side the digital. Due to events, physical treasure hunt had to be removed which had changed the narrative and priorities. Therefor, we had to incorporate AR in a different way, and make the website the main part of the game.

Project Challanges

Our biggest challenge was adapting our project to the new conditions of social distancing & coordinating time

  • • Converting all physical & content portions of the treasure hunt into digital
  • • Keeping track of what's complete and incomplete
  • • Adjusting Narrative to new situation
  • • Deciding on what to Prioritize
  • • Understanding what's necessary and not

Solutions

  • • Making the treasure hunt mainly on the website
  • • Using project management web application such as freed camp
  • • Going through each chapter (that was already completed) and make adjustment to create more impact
  • • Shrinking chapters & Jumping to less platforms
  • • Updating and communicating on the progress

Our Goal

  • A consistent design that follows the theme of curiosity, hope & magic Giving students a break, relief stress and provide them with a sense of hope.

User Goal

  • Cause mischief, follow their curiosity, decode riddles to unlock a mysterious box.

Objectives

  • • Feeding into users curiosity.
  • • Providing something to motivate the user to play ( awards)
  • • Giving a sense of hope when they complete the game.
  • • Incorporating AR as a vital but also optional portion

Target Audience

Highschool to College Students.

These students would generally be stressed, feeling hopeless, worried and require a break. They enjoy collecting, accessorizing and are generally a curious person.

Design

Highschool to College Students.

Following a theme of magic and chaos. We used two different colors to represent two different characters and the mood they convey. It also falls in line with their personality. The witch prefers pastel happy colors, and is generally a good witch. However, the wizard is very studious and uses the darker colors. In addition, magic was a good part of many childhoods, and we would like to bring that innocence and hope back
